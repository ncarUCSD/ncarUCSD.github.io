Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to some of our most common questions

+
Mask Share is a peer-to-peer platform to help people with spare masks send them directly to healthcare workers on the front lines who need them now. Many hospitals are limiting the number of masks doctors and nurses can use due to limited quantities on hand and saving up for the anticipated peak, and many are forced to go without due to lack of supply. For example, Dr Samantha Batt-Rawden, the president of the Doctors’ Association UK (DAUK) states “Lack of personal protective equipment continues to be a critical issue. It is heartbreaking to hear that some staff have been told to simply ‘hold their breath’ due to lack of masks. We find this concerning and want to do our best to contribute to the NHS during this difficult period.
+
People in the United Kingdom have masks in their closets, garages, basements, and workshops, and can get linked up with a healthcare worker near them who needs masks now. If you are a healthcare worker, fill out this form so we know who you are and where we need to send your masks. If you have masks, fill out this form, and we’ll email you with a healthcare worker match so you can ship your masks directly to them. Mask Share will make sure to verify the identities of the healthcare workers before making any matches. These masks are not for purchase or for sale. Those who are matched with a healthcare worker are expected to cover the cost of shipping & handling.
